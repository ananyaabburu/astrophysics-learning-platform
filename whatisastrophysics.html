What is Astrophysics?

Astrophysics is the branch of science that studies how the universe works. It combines astronomy, physics, mathematics, and computer science to understand objects and phenomena in space.

What Do Astrophysicists Study?

Astrophysicists study stars, planets, galaxies, black holes, dark matter, and the evolution of the universe.

Why Is Astrophysics Important?

Astrophysics helps us understand the origins of the universe and the physical laws that govern everything from tiny particles to entire galaxies.

Interesting Facts

  • The universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old.
  • Light from distant galaxies can take billions of years to reach Earth.
  • Astrophysics combines multiple scientific disciplines.
