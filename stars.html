Stars

Stars are massive balls of hot gas that produce energy through nuclear fusion. They are the building blocks of galaxies and play an important role in the universe.

How Do Stars Produce Energy?

Inside a star's core, hydrogen atoms combine to form helium. This process releases enormous amounts of heat and light.

Life Cycle of a Star

Stars are born in clouds of gas and dust, spend most of their lives producing energy, and eventually evolve into different objects depending on their mass.

Interesting Facts

  • The Sun is a star.
  • Some stars are hundreds of times larger than the Sun.
  • Stars can live for billions of years.
